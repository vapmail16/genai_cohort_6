## 1. Executive summary

HelixFlow Technologies (2,400 employees, B2B workflow SaaS) announced on **2026-06-12** that all software engineers must adopt **"Helix Copilot"** (OpenAI-compatible enterprise assistant) for code review, documentation, and incident triage by **2026-07-01**. Non-engineering staff may opt in. The policy cites a **30% productivity target** and alignment with three enterprise customers who require AI-assisted SDLC attestations in RFPs.

CEO **Morgan Chen** framed the move as "table stakes for 2027 renewals." CTO **Priya Nair** said opt-out requests would be reviewed case-by-case for regulated workloads only.

---

## 2. Stated policy (HR memo excerpt)

- Engineering org (approx. **520 FTE**) must enable Copilot on corporate SSO by July 1.
- Code merged to `main` should include Copilot-assisted review notes in PR templates (honor system, first quarter).
- **Security**: no customer PII in prompts; use approved redaction plugin.
- **IP**: outputs are company-owned; employees may not train external models on HelixFlow repos.
- Violations: manager escalation → performance plan (wording in memo).
